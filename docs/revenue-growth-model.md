# Revenue Growth Model - DV Regression Lab

Updated: 2026-05-30

This note translates the archived proof into a current, ethical revenue path. It keeps the repository's archived status visible while preserving the commercial idea behind the work.

## Model

| Field | Decision |
|---|---|
| Revenue type | Archived proof to diagnostic, workshop, enablement, pilot, or partner package |
| Primary reader or buyer | ASIC verification managers, EDA platform teams, CI owners, and silicon program leads. |
| Wedge | DV teams need a clearer loop from nightly regression evidence to flaky-test triage and operator handoff. |
| Operating note | Sell the revived workflow or advisory package; do not sell an unsupported archive as a live product. |

## Offer Ladder

| Offer | Price assumption | Scope |
|---|---|---|
| Regression triage assessment | $8k-$20k | Review current regression logs, flake posture, and evidence retention gaps. |
| DV control-tower pilot | $40k-$150k | Adapt the scheduler, classifier, and evidence pack to one verification block. |
| EDA workflow extension | $100k+/year | License or maintain the triage workflow as part of a platform toolchain. |

## Activation Loop

- Traffic source: Semiconductor engineering articles.
- First action: read the README status note and one proof path that can be checked quickly.
- Conversion offer: Regression triage assessment.
- Expansion: convert a diagnostic or workshop into a scoped pilot, enablement pack, or maintained partner asset.

## Growth Experiments

- Write a DV flake-cost calculator
- Create a sample nightly-regression walkthrough
- Offer a paid assessment to one verification team

## Metrics

- Assessment calls
- Nightly triage time saved
- Flake detection precision
- Pilot renewal

## Ethical Boundary

- Do not claim silicon design ownership
- Keep customer logs out of public artifacts
- Avoid production readiness claims without partner validation
- Keep archived status visible in sales or portfolio conversations.
- Do not invent users, revenue, endorsements, certifications, or customer traction.
- Use buyer psychology only to reduce confusion and risk, not to pressure users.
